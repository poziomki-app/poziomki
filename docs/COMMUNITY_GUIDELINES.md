# Community Guidelines

Poziomki is a space for university students to make genuine connections. These guidelines help keep our community safe and welcoming.

---

## Our Values

**Authenticity** — Be yourself. Use real photos and accurate information.

**Respect** — Treat others as you'd want to be treated.

**Safety** — Look out for yourself and others.

**Offline-first** — The goal is real friendships, not endless scrolling.

---

## The Golden Rule

**Would you say or do this in person, with your name attached?**

If not, don't do it here.

---

## What's Welcome

- Genuine profiles with real photos
- Respectful conversations
- Organizing meetups and events
- Sharing interests and hobbies
- Making friends across programs and departments
- Constructive feedback about the app

---

## What's Not Allowed

### Harassment & Bullying
- Threats, intimidation, or stalking
- Repeated unwanted contact after being asked to stop
- Mocking, insulting, or belittling others
- Coordinated harassment of any user
- Doxxing (sharing private information)

**Consequence:** Immediate suspension or permanent ban

### Hate & Discrimination
- Attacks based on race, ethnicity, religion, gender, sexual orientation, disability, or other protected characteristics
- Hate symbols or slurs
- Promoting extremist ideologies

**Consequence:** Immediate permanent ban

### Inappropriate Content
- Sexually explicit or pornographic material
- Violence or gore
- Illegal activities
- Self-harm or suicide promotion

**Consequence:** Content removal, suspension, or ban depending on severity

### Deception
- Fake profiles or impersonation
- Misleading photos (old photos, photos of others)
- Lying about being a student
- Catfishing
- Scams or fraud

**Consequence:** Account termination

### Spam & Abuse
- Commercial advertising or promotion
- Repetitive or bulk messaging
- Recruiting for MLM, cults, or similar
- Malicious links or phishing

**Consequence:** Account termination

### Platform Abuse
- Creating multiple accounts
- Circumventing bans or suspensions
- Exploiting bugs or vulnerabilities (report them instead!)
- Scraping or automated access

**Consequence:** Account termination, possible legal action

## Event Guidelines

### For Organizers
- Provide accurate event details (time, place, what to expect)
- Clearly state if there are costs involved
- Don't cancel without notifying attendees
- Be present at your own events
- Ensure events are legal and safe

### For Attendees
- RSVP honestly (don't sign up if you won't attend)
- Respect the event organizer's rules
- Arrive on time or communicate delays
- Leave events that make you uncomfortable

### Event Red Flags
Report events that:
- Seem designed to isolate or manipulate people
- Promote illegal activities
- Are fronts for commercial, political, or religious recruitment
- Request unusual personal information

---

## Messaging Etiquette

### Do:
- Start with a friendly introduction
- Reference shared interests
- Accept "no" gracefully
- Give people time to respond

### Don't:
- Send explicit messages or images
- Spam the same message to multiple people
- Continue messaging after being ignored or blocked
- Request personal information too quickly (phone, address, social media)

### If Someone Makes You Uncomfortable
1. You can ignore or block them
2. Report concerning behavior
3. You don't owe anyone a response

---

## Safety Tips

### Online
- Don't share personal details (address, phone, schedule) too quickly
- Be skeptical of requests for money or personal favors
- Trust your instincts — if something feels off, it probably is

### Meeting Offline
- Meet in public places first
- Tell a friend where you're going and when
- Arrange your own transportation
- Keep your phone charged
- Have an exit plan
- Trust your gut — leave if uncomfortable

### Red Flags
- Pressure to meet immediately
- Avoiding video calls or public meetings
- Asking for money or financial help
- Inconsistent stories
- Refusing to share basic information

## Reporting

### How to Report
1. **In-app:** Tap the menu (...) on any profile, message, or event and select "Report"
2. **Email:** safety@poziomki.app for urgent issues

### What Happens When You Report
1. We review the report (usually within 24-48 hours)
2. We take action if guidelines are violated
3. We notify you of the outcome (without revealing specifics about actions taken)
4. Serious cases may involve university authorities or law enforcement

### False Reports
Deliberately false reports are themselves a violation and may result in suspension.

---

## Enforcement

| Violation | Typical Consequence |
|-----------|---------------------|
| Minor first offense | Warning |
| Repeated minor offenses | Temporary suspension (1-7 days) |
| Serious offense | Extended suspension (30 days) or ban |
| Severe offense (threats, hate, illegal content) | Immediate permanent ban |
| Criminal activity | Ban + report to authorities |

We consider:
- Severity of the violation
- Whether it's a first offense
- Impact on other users
- Whether behavior was intentional

### Appeals
If you believe a decision was wrong, email appeals@poziomki.app with:
- Your account email
- Date of the action
- Why you believe it was an error

We review appeals within 7 days.
